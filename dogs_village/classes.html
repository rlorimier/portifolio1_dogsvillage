What We Offer

We understand that the individual needs of dogs and their owners vary so we offer a full range of services to suit your training needs. We offer private lessons at our centre along with our very popular group class options. Our class schedule has something for all dogs, no matter what age or breed.


Puppy Group Classes

We’re often asked what the most important thing is to know about raising a dog, and our answer is always the same: Start training early!
This course has been designed for pups under 6 months of age. Over the 4 weeks of this course, you and your pup will learn all about how to share your home together and get you on the right track from the start. We will teach you the basics like "sit", "down", "stay", "recall", "lead work" and "leave it" but the most important thing we will show you is how to build the bond between your family and your new pet in a safe and ethical way. Our classes will help your puppy develop into a well-mannered and social addition to the family. All of our course attendees will be emailed class notes and videos after each class to help with their training at home. We use only scientific and welfare-friendly methods of training and our team are always to help by email if you find that you are struggling with any of the training.

What you need to bring along to class:

All we ask is that you bring along your dog along with their regular collar/harness and lead. Please note, we do not allow the use of slip leads, choke chains, prong/pinch collars or e collars in the centre. We will provide any equipment needed plus lots of yummy treats. If your dog has special dietary needs please do bring along a portion of their food. We ask that you do not feed your dog for at least 2 hours before class is due to start.


Basic Training

Whether or not you plan on competing in dog sports and events, it’s critical that all dogs receive basic training, from how to walk well on leash to being comfortable with strangers.
These are suitable for complete beginners and puppy follow-ons. All ages are welcome, as long as your dog is comfortable around other dogs and owners. Lead by our experienced and accredited trainers we will give you those all-important skills to develop your bond and communication with your dog. We will help your dog become a well-mannered, friendly addition to the family. Individual Training

You’re not alone: Most dog owners will face some kind of behavior issue in a dog’s lifetime. Get solutions to some of the most common nuisances, from barking and whining to fears like loud noise.
Suitable for complete beginners at all ages that would like to learn without distractions or if you prefer something personal and tailored to you and your dog and prefer the convenience of training at a time that suits you. The training session will last an hour and a half, and our trainer will work with you on the practical elements needed to help you and your dog. Following this, you will be emailed a training plan tailored towards what you and your dog need to follow going forward.
Aggressive Dogs Welcome - Private socialization, obedience & training for aggressive, shy, or wild dogs. We use scientific behavior analysis to determine what is the trigger for the dog's unwanted behavior. We use a combination of management, alternative behaviors, and calming techniques to change behavior.


Training for Dog Sports - Agility

It’s one of the best ways to deeply bond with your dog. It’s great exercise. And it just might be one of the most personally rewarding endeavors of your life. As you and your dog develop the skills necessary for each sport, you become a true team in every sense of the word. Whether you just train for fun, or actually compete, we guarantee that you’ll feel a sense of pride at seeing your dog in their “happy” place, showing off new skills and accomplishing incredible goals — together.
Suitable for dogs at all ages with basic training and energetic owners. Dog agility is a sport where you direct your dog through a pre-set obstacle course within a certain time limit. Courses typically have between 14-20 obstacles, which can include tunnels, weave poles, tire jumps, seesaws, and pause tables where the dog must stop for a set amount of time. At each trial you and your dog will race around the unique courses designed for that day. All of this is done with your dog relying solely on the cues and body language you use to direct them on course.
